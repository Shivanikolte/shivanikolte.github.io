The belief that the four natural elements (earth, air, fire, and water) can affect mood is rooted in ancient philosophy and mythology. While there is no scientific evidence to support this idea, some people may find that being in the presence of or interacting with these elements can have a calming or uplifting effect. Here are some ways that each element may affect mood:

Water

The sound and movement of water, such as waves or a running stream, can have a calming effect on the mind and body. Taking a bath or shower can also be a relaxing and rejuvenating experience.

Fire

The warmth and light of a fire can create a cozy and comforting atmosphere, which can help to promote relaxation and calmness. Candlelight or a fireplace can be especially soothing in the evening.

Air

Fresh air and good ventilation can help to improve mood and cognitive function.
